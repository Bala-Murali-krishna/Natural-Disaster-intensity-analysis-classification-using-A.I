Earthquake

An earthquake is a sudden shaking of the ground caused by the release of energy stored in Earth’s crust. These seismic waves are produced when masses of rock straining against one another suddenly fracture and “slip”. Earthquakes can range in intensity and occur most often along geologic faults, narrow zones where rock masses move in relation to one another. The major fault lines of the world are located at the fringes of the huge tectonic plates that make up Earth’s crust.

Cyclone

A cyclone is a large air mass that rotates around a strong center of low atmospheric pressure. In the Northern Hemisphere, it rotates counterclockwise, while in the Southern Hemisphere, it rotates clockwise. Cyclones are characterized by inward-spiraling winds that rotate about a zone of low pressure. The largest low-pressure systems are polar vortices and extratropical cyclones of the largest scale. Warm-core cyclones such as tropical cyclones and subtropical cyclones also lie within the synoptic scale. Cyclones can transition between extratropical, subtropical, and tropical phases.

Floods

A flood is an overflow of water that submerges land that is usually dry. Floods can occur due to an overflow of water from water bodies, such as a river, lake, or ocean, or due to an accumulation of rainwater on saturated ground. Floods can cause damage to homes and businesses if they are in the natural flood plains of rivers. Human changes to the environment often increase the intensity and frequency of flooding, for example land use changes such as deforestation and removal of wetlands, changes in waterway course or flood controls such as with levees, and larger environmental issues such as climate change and sea level rise.

WildFire

A wildfire is an unplanned, uncontrolled, and unpredictable fire in an area of combustible vegetation. Wildfires can occur in forests, grasslands, brushlands, or lands sown to crops. The terms forest fire, brush fire, etc., may be used to describe specific types of wildfires; their usage varies according to the characteristics of the fire and the region in which it occurs. Wildfires can have both beneficial and detrimental effects on the environment. Some natural forest ecosystems depend on wildfire, while human societies can be severely impacted by fires.
